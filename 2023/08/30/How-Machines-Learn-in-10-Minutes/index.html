Analyze the learning process

  1. You started with no experiences with aliens
  2. From the experience of hanging out with Bob, you collected initial experience.

    Experience

  3. Then from the experience of hanging out with Patricia, you concluded a pattern: aliens are good at eating spicy stuffs

    Inductive Reasoning $\rightarrow$ Pattern

  4. When you were hanging out with Op, you used your pattern, developed an expectation that Op is just as good at handling spiciness as Bob and Patricia.

    Deductive Reasoning $\rightarrow$ Prediction

  5. But it turns out that the reality contradicts with the guess! Which means the pattern is not correct!

    Comparing Prediction with Reality

  6. You reflected on this contradiction and updates your pattern: most aliens are good at eating spicy foods

    Reflection $\rightarrow$ Update Pattern

Overview: how machines recognize digits

To begin with, we will learn about supervised learning. Supervised learning requires 2 things to train with: question and answers.

Questions can be in any form, in this example they are images of handwritten digits (which corresponds to new data).

Answers, on the other hand, are the actual results. In this example, they are digits 0~9.

Note that in practice we usually use x_train and x_test to represent questions. Similarly y_train and y_test for the answers.
